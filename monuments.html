Historical Monuments in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering 73-meter-high tower located in Delhi, India. It was built in the early 13th century as a victory tower and is the tallest brick minaret in the world

India GATE

The Ajanta Caves are a series of 29 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments located in Maharashtra, India. They were built between the 2nd century BC and the 6th century AD and are famous for their wall paintings.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort located in Delhi, India. It was built in the mid-17th century and served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors for nearly 200 years. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
